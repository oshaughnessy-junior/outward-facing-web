### Concept

The study of gravitational waves allows us to probe the most extreme environments in the universe, such as the mergers of black holes and neutron stars. By developing high-precision tools to interpret these signals, we can test the limits of General Relativity and build a comprehensive census of the compact objects that populate our cosmos. As detectors increase in sensitivity, we are moving from the era of first discoveries to a precision era of "data-driven astrophysics."

### Research Topics

- **Advanced Parameter Inference**: Developing rapid and robust tools to extract source properties from complex waveforms.
  - [Pankow et al., "Novel scheme for rapid parallel parameter estimation of gravitational waves from compact binary coalescences"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/arXiv:1502.04370/abstract)
- **Direct NR Comparisons**: Comparing observations directly with full Numerical Relativity simulations to identify complex dynamics like orbital eccentricity.
  - [Healy et al., "Second RIT binary black hole simulations catalog and its application to gravitational waves parameter estimation"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2019PhRvD.100b4021H/abstract)
  - [Gayathri et al., "Eccentricity estimate for black hole mergers with numerical relativity simulations"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2020arXiv200905461G/abstract)
- **LVK Leadership**: Leading the interpretation of observing runs and the discovery of new binary classes.
  - [The LIGO Scientific Collaboration, "The population of merging compact binaries inferred using gravitational waves through GWTC-3"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2021arXiv211103634T/abstract)
  - [Abbott et al., "Observation of Gravitational Waves from Two Neutron Star-Black Hole Coalescences"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2021ApJ...915L...5A/abstract)

### Impact

Major contributions to gravitational wave observations, both within LIGO Scientific Collaboration (e.g., O3 "populations" paper; O3a catalog; NSBH discovery) and outside (e.g., inferring eccentricity for 190521), with multiple RIT students. Provides science leadership and infrastructure to coalition of partners using our frameworks for source and population inference.
