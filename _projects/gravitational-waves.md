Development of high-precision frameworks for the observation and interpretation of gravitational waves from compact binary coalescences, focusing on parameter estimation and numerical relativity comparisons.

### Research Topics
- **Parameter Inference**: [Pankow et al., "Novel scheme for rapid parallel parameter estimation of gravitational waves from compact binary coalescences"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/arXiv:1502.04370/abstract)
- **NR Comparisons**: [Healy et al., "Second RIT binary black hole simulations catalog and its application to gravitational waves parameter estimation"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2019PhRvD.100b4021H/abstract), [Gayathri et al., "Eccentricity estimate for black hole mergers with numerical relativity simulations"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2020arXiv200905461G/abstract)
- **LVK O3 Results**: [The LIGO Scientific Collaboration et al., "The population of merging compact binaries inferred using gravitational waves through GWTC-3"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2021arXiv211103634T/abstract), [Abbott et al., "Observation of Gravitational Waves from Two Neutron Star-Black Hole Coalescences"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2021ApJ...915L...5A/abstract)

### Impact
- Major contributions to gravitational wave observations within the LIGO Scientific Collaboration (e.g., O3 populations paper, O3a catalog, NSBH discovery).
- Inference of eccentricity for event GW190521.
- Provision of science leadership and infrastructure for partner coalitions using RIT frameworks for source and population inference.
