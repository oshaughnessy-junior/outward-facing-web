### Concept
Some of the most violent events in the universe produce both gravitational waves and electromagnetic radiation (light). By jointly interpreting these signals, we can gain a complete picture of the merger process, from the internal physics of the colliding objects to the chemical elements they forge and eject into space.

### Research Topics
- **Joint GW-EM Inference**: Developing tools to interpret gravitational wave signals and kilonova light curves simultaneously.
  - [Ristic et al., "Joint Electromagnetic and Gravitational Wave Inference of Binary Neutron Star Merger GW170817 Using Forward-Modeling Ejecta Predictions"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2025arXiv251212130A/abstract)
- **Nuclear Equation of State (EOS)**: Using relic r-process abundances and joint observations to constrain the properties of nuclear matter.
  - [Holmbeck et al., "A Nuclear Equation of State Inferred from Stellar r-process Abundances"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2022ApJ...926..196H/abstract)
  - [Al-Mamun et al., "Combining Electromagnetic and Gravitational-Wave Constraints on Neutron-Star Masses and Radii"](#)
- **Kilonova Modeling**: Using neural networks and anisotropic radiative transfer to model kilonova light curves.
  - [Peng et al., "Kilonova Light-Curve Interpolation with Neural Networks"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2024arXiv240205871P/abstract)

### Impact
Advance overall understanding of light emitted from neutron star mergers, and its implications, with RIT students (Ristic, D. Fernando), external undergraduate (Peng), and LANL collaborators.
