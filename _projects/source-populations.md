Investigation of the formation and evolution of compact binary populations, utilizing iterative inference and astrophysically motivated models to disentangle various formation channels.

### Research Topics
- **Population Inference**: [Wysocki et al., "Reconstructing phenomenological distributions of compact binaries via gravitational wave observations"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2019PhRvD.100d3012W/abstract)
- **AGN Disk Models**: [Gayathri et al., "Gravitational Wave Source Populations: Disentangling an AGN Component"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2023ApJ...945L..29G/abstract)
- **Simulation-Based Inference**: [Delfavero et al., "Iteratively Comparing Gravitational-Wave Observations to the Evolution of Massive Stellar Binaries"](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2023arXiv230305436D/abstract)

### Impact
- Introduced iterative inference for compact binary populations.
- Advanced new AGN disk models for the formation of binary black holes.
